# Spellfire Tournament Rules v2.0

> These rules are based on the 4th Edition rules with tournament modifications.
> Card special powers always take precedence over these rules.

---

## Notable Rule Changes from 4th Edition

- When a player runs out of cards, they reshuffle their discard pile into a new draw pile **at the end of the current turn**.
- Everyone may include one "free" avatar in their deck — its level doesn't count toward the level cap, though it still counts as one card.
- Events that are played are sent to the **Void**. Events discarded before being played are sent to the **Abyss**.

---

## The Cards

Each card has an icon in the upper left corner identifying its type. The icon may contain a number representing the card's level — the higher, the more powerful.

**Champion types** — all follow rules for "champions": Clerics, Heroes, Monsters, Wizards, Psionicists, Thieves, Regents.

Some champions and allies have `?` for a level, meaning their level is unknown until conditions on the card are met.

---

## Worlds

The world logo on a card ties it to a specific game world. Only champions, artifacts, realms, and holdings carry world logos. World logos affect which cards work together — for example, an AD&D champion can only use an AD&D artifact.

**Worlds:** AD&D, Birthright, Dark Sun, Dragonlance, Forgotten Realms, Greyhawk, Ravenloft.

---

## Special Powers

The text box on each card contains its special power — an ability that falls outside the normal rules.

- **Racial and movement characteristics** (elf, flyer, undead) are _not_ special powers and cannot be stripped by cards that remove special powers.
- Cards that specifically strip away a characteristic (e.g., the ability to fly) work normally.

---

No individual card may appear more than once in a deck unless that card explicitly states otherwise. A card is considered the same if it has both the same icon and the same name.

## Dungeon Cards

Dungeon cards are a free card — they don't count toward deck size (making actual deck sizes 56, 76, or 111). At the start of the game, each player places their dungeon card directly into play. It is not shuffled into the deck.

**Removing a dungeon card:**

1. A **Wish spell** — removes both the Wish and the caster from the game, along with the dungeon card.
2. **Gib Kcir** (16/RR Chase) — can send the dungeon card to the Abyss; Gib Kcir herself goes to the Abyss.
3. **Attacking the dungeon card** directly during Phase 4 (in lieu of attacking a realm). The owner must have at least one realm in play (razed or not) for the dungeon to be attackable.

If the attacker wins, the victorious champion is removed from the game. Attached cards go to the discard pile. The attacker earns **dungeon spoils**: draw one card — if it's a realm, it goes to the Abyss and the victor may reshuffle their discard pile; otherwise it's treated as a normal spoils of victory. If the defender wins, the defender earns dungeon spoils and no further combat may occur that turn over the dungeon.

Once removed from the game, a dungeon card cannot return.

---

## Starting the Game

1. Each player draws (or cuts to) one card. The player with the highest last digit of the card number goes first. Ties are redrawn.
2. Each player shuffles their deck and places it face down. The player to their right may cut it.
3. Players draw their starting hand.

All players draw their starting hand on their first turn regardless of any cards that limit draws.

### Hand Size by Deck Size

| Deck Size | Starting Hand | Draw Per Turn | Max Hand |
| --------- | ------------- | ------------- | -------- |
| 55-card   | 5             | 3             | 8        |
| 75-card   | 6             | 4             | 10       |
| 110-card  | 7             | 5             | 12       |

### Mulligan

If a player has not played a realm and has no realm in hand by the end of their **second turn**, the game may be restarted. Requirements:

- The player must have played any helpful events (like Good Fortune) that might have helped them find a realm.
- The player must reveal their hand to prove they have no realms.
- A mulligan cannot be called if the player voluntarily discarded a realm.
- The same player may call a mulligan at most **twice in a row**. On the third game, they must continue regardless.

When restarting, determine who goes first again.

---

## Victory

**Standard (55 and 75-card):** First player to have **6 unrazed realms** in their formation wins.

**110-card:** First player to have **10 unrazed realms** wins.

---

## Turn Sequence

Phases 1, 5, and 6 are **mandatory**. Phases 0, 2, 3, and 4 are optional.

### Phase 0 — Play a Rule Card / Start-of-Turn Actions

Perform any actions that must happen at the beginning of a turn (e.g., Marco Volo lets the player look at an opponent's top card). Rule cards are played here.

**Rule cards:** Change the rules of the game for all players while in play. Playing a new rule card discards the previous one.

### Phase 1 — Draw Cards

Draw the number of cards indicated for the deck size. Cards granting extra draws do so here unless another phase is specified.

### Phase 2 — Play a Realm and/or Holding

Only one realm and one holding can be played per turn. Steps A and B can be performed in any order.

**A) Play, rebuild, or replace one realm** — only one of the three options per turn.

**B) Play one holding** — must be attached to a realm from the same world. Once attached, holdings cannot be rearranged.

**Rebuilding a razed realm:** Discard 3 cards from hand during Phase 2 to flip a razed realm face-up. This forfeits the ability to play a new realm that turn. The realm's special power is reactivated.

**Replacing a razed realm:** Play a new realm on top of a razed one. The razed realm is discarded.

### Phase 3 — Pool and Spells

The active player may bounce back and forth between steps A and B freely. Only the active player acts during this phase.

**A) Place any number of champions, artifacts, and magical items into the pool.**

- Artifacts must be attached to a champion from the same world (one artifact per champion).
- Any number of magical items may be attached to any champion.
- Once attached, artifacts and magical items cannot be rearranged.

**B) Use any Phase 3 spell, psionic power, blood ability, or thief skill.**

- A champion in the pool must be able to use the card.
- Other players may respond.

### Phase 4 — Attack One Realm

The active player may attack one realm belonging to another player. See the **Combat** section for full detail.

### Phase 5 — Phase 5 Cards and Hand Adjustment

Champions may no longer be placed in the pool, and magical items may no longer be attached (unless obtained as spoils of victory).

**A) Use any card playable in Phase 5.** A champion in the pool must be able to use it.

**B) Discard down to maximum hand size.** Events discarded this way go to the Abyss; all other cards go to the discard pile.

### Phase 6 — End Turn

Rap on the table to end your turn. Play passes to the left.

## Rule of the Cosmos

Only one of each named champion, artifact, realm, or holding card can be in play at once. While any player has a specific card in play, no other player may bring that same card into play.

- **Same name + same icon = same card** for this rule.
- Razed realms are still considered in play.
- Champions in Limbo are **not** considered in play.

---

## The Formation

Realms are arranged in a **pyramid** with its base toward the owning player:

```
        [A]
      [B] [C]
    [D] [E] [F]
```

- **A** protects B and C.
- **B** protects D and E.
- **C** protects E and F.
- **E** is protected by both B and C.

A realm cannot be attacked if an **unrazed realm** is in front of it. Razed realms offer no protection — realms behind them become exposed.

**Playing realms:** Must fill positions in order. A must have a card (even if razed) before B or C can be filled. B and C must both be filled before D, E, or F. Within a row, order doesn't matter.

**Empty positions:** If cardplay forces a realm to be discarded, the empty spot must be filled before any further realms can be played behind it.

**No realms in formation:** If a player has no realms at all (razed or unrazed) at the end of the current player's turn, all champions in that player's pool are discarded.

---

## Razed Realms

A razed realm is turned **face down** in place. It is not removed from its position.

- Any holding attached to the realm is discarded.
- The realm's special power is eliminated (unless it activates on being razed).
- A razed realm is still considered "in play" for the Rule of the Cosmos.
- A razed realm can be rebuilt during Phase 2 by discarding 3 cards from hand.
- A razed realm is discarded when a new realm is played on top of it.

---

## The Pool

The pool is an area (typically to the right of the formation) where champions, artifacts, and magical items in play are kept. Cards in the pool:

- Are considered **in play**.
- Do not count against the player's hand size.
- Stay there until used in battle or forced out by cardplay.
- If a champion is discarded, all attached cards go to the discard pile.

A champion in the pool may already have artifacts and magical items attached when it enters combat.

---

## Zones

| Zone                 | Description                                                                                                            |
| -------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Hand**             | Cards held by the player, not yet in play.                                                                             |
| **Draw Pile**        | The face-down deck. Cards are drawn from here.                                                                         |
| **Discard Pile**     | Where most cards go when removed from play. Can be reshuffled into a new draw pile when the draw pile runs out.        |
| **Pool / Formation** | "In play" — cards actively in the game.                                                                                |
| **Limbo**            | Temporary removal. Champions typically return at the end of their owner's next turn. Cards in Limbo are not "in play." |
| **Abyss**            | Cards sent here by specific effects, or events discarded before being played. Some cards can retrieve from the Abyss.  |
| **Void**             | Permanently removed from the game. Events go here after being played. Nothing can be retrieved from the Void.          |

**Limbo notes:**

- A champion sent to Limbo **during combat** loses all attached cards (sent to discard).
- A champion sent to Limbo **outside of combat** retains all attachments.
- If another player brings an identical champion into play, the Limbo champion is discarded when it would return.

---

## Running Out of Cards

When a player's draw pile is depleted, they reshuffle their discard pile into a new draw pile **at the end of the current turn**. Cards that would have been drawn while the pile was empty are simply lost.

Cards in Limbo, the Abyss, or the Void are not included in the reshuffle.

---

## Combat

During Phase 4, the active player may attack **one realm** of any other player. All attacks against a single realm constitute a **battle**. A battle may last multiple **rounds**, with each round pitting one attacking champion against one defending champion.

### Combat Sequence (One Round)

**A) Attacker declares the target realm.**

Must be a realm the attacker can legally reach (exposed, or accessible via special movement power).

**B) Attacker presents an attacking champion.**

From hand or pool. No cards may be added to the champion at this moment (pool champions may already have attachments). A champion can only participate in one round per battle (unless their power states otherwise).

> **World bonus:** If the attacking champion is from the same world as the attacked realm, add **+3** to their adjusted level.

**C) Defender presents a defending champion** (or declines).

From hand or pool. No new cards may be added at this moment (pool champions may already have attachments). If no champion is presented, the realm is razed immediately — battle is over.

> **World bonus:** If the defending champion is from the same world as the attacked realm, add **+3** to their adjusted level.

**D) Attacker's special power and attached cards activate first, then defender's.**

See [Order of Activation](#order-of-activation) for the precise sequence. If the attacker wins through their special power before the defender activates, the defender is discarded and the round ends.

**E) Compare adjusted levels.**

The side with the higher total is winning. A tie means the defender is winning.

**F) The losing player may play one card.**

Compare levels again. The losing player continues playing cards one at a time (re-comparing after each) until they are winning, or until they cannot or choose not to play another card.

- The **losing player** may play any legal card.
- The **winning player** may only play **events** or **"play at any time"** cards during this phase.

**G) Highest adjusted level wins. Ties go to the defender.**

**H) Winner takes their champion (with attached magical items and artifacts) back to their pool.**

Allies, spells, and other non-permanent cards used in combat are discarded by the winner. The loser discards all cards used in combat, including the champion.

**I) After a round ends:**

- If the attacker **won** but the realm was **not razed**: the attacker may send a different champion (not previously used this battle) against the same realm for another round. Cannot switch to a different realm.
- If the attacker **lost**, or the realm was **razed or discarded**: the battle is over. The attacker may not attack any other realm for the rest of this turn.

**J) Spoils of victory:**

- **Attacker** draws one card from their draw pile if the attacked realm was **razed or discarded** during the battle.
- **Defender** draws one card from their draw pile if an attacking champion was **defeated and discarded** during the battle.

The spoils card may be played immediately, held in hand, or returned to the top of the draw pile. Playing a spoils allows cards that normally can't be played at that time (like a realm during Phase 4).

### Order of Activation

When a combat round begins, cards activate in this order:

1. Rule card
2. Dungeon card
3. Realm power
4. Holding power
5. Spells, psionic powers, and other cards in play before the battle (in the order played)
6. Powers that activate "before combat"
7. **Attacker's** special power and attached cards:
   - a. Artifacts
   - b. Allies
   - c. Magical items
   - d. Blood abilities
   - e. Thief skills
   - f. Unarmed combat cards
   - g. Psionic power cards
   - h. Spells
8. **Defender's** special power and attached cards (same order as attacker)

_Within the same card type, cards with higher bonus modifiers activate first. If bonus is equal, alphabetical order by card name. Same-name cards with no bonus: lowest card number goes first._

### Ending the Battle

| Situation                                        | Result                                                              |
| ------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Defender defeats and discards attacking champion | Battle over. Defender earns spoils.                                 |
| Attacker wins round, realm not razed             | Attacker may send a new champion for another round.                 |
| Defender declines to defend                      | Realm is razed. Battle over. Attacker earns spoils.                 |
| Attacker declines to send a champion             | Battle over. No spoils for either side.                             |
| Cardplay razes/discards realm during combat      | Battle over. Attacker earns spoils. Both champions return to pools. |
| Cardplay discards both champions                 | Battle over. No winner. No spoils.                                  |
| Cardplay discards attacking champion             | Battle over. Defender earns spoils.                                 |

### World Bonuses

When a champion attacks or defends a realm from the **same world**, that champion's adjusted level is increased by **+3**.

### Wall Spells

If cardplay prevents an attacking champion from continuing to fight, that champion is **defeated but returns to the pool** (not discarded). The defender does not earn spoils. The battle ends.

If cardplay prevents a defending champion from continuing to fight, that round ends. The defender's champion returns to the pool. The attacker may send a new champion for another round.

An attacking champion always has the opportunity to play one card to continue the battle (such as the Fly spell to bypass a wall), regardless of current combat totals.

## Icon Abilities

Each champion type has an icon ability:

| Champion Type | Icon Ability                                                |
| ------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Wizard        | Cast wizard spells                                          |
| Cleric        | Cast cleric spells; immune to Fear of Undead                |
| Psionicist    | Use psionic power cards                                     |
| Regent        | Use blood ability cards                                     |
| Thief         | Use thief skill cards                                       |
| Hero          | Use unarmed combat cards                                    |
| Monster       | No icon power; can use magical items and artifacts normally |

Removing a champion's special power does **not** remove their icon ability. Changing the champion's type changes their icon ability.

---

## Casting Spells

Spells are either:

- **Offensive** — directly affect the opposing card
- **Defensive** — only affect the caster's champion or friendly cards

Spells are cast during the phase listed on the card. A spell must have a valid target to be cast.

Spells cast by only one player's champion type require that champion to be available (in pool or in combat) to cast them.

### Dispel Magic, Spell Turning, and Reflection

These spells can be cast **at any time in response to another spell being cast**.

- **Dispel Magic** — works against offensive or defensive spells.
- **Spell Turning** — works only against offensive spells.
- **Reflection** — works only against offensive spells; when cast in Phase 4, it continuously reflects spells back at the caster.

### Wish Spell

The most powerful spell. Overrides all immunities except explicit Wish immunity. The caster becomes incapable of further action (casting spells, using attachments) after casting Wish, unless the attachment provides continuous immunity.

Wish can only negate events that have a **stated duration**; it cannot stop instantaneous events.

---

## Instant-Defeat Cards

Some cards instantly defeat a specific champion type in combat. When such an instant-defeat is triggered:

- The player about to be defeated may play **one card** to avoid the defeat.
- That card must **change the condition of defeat** (e.g., change the champion type).
- Cards cannot be played to target the source of the defeat.
- The special powers of allies, artifacts, and magical items do not count for the instant-defeat rule.

---

## Immunities

A champion immune to a card type is immune to **offensive powers only**, unless a defensive immunity is explicitly noted.

- Immunities are **always active**, regardless of Order of Activation.
- Applies to all cards in play — not to cards in Limbo, Abyss, or hand.
- If a power only affects the attached champion (not the opposing champion), immunity to it is not negated by the opposing champion's immunity.

---

## Events

Events can be played **at any time** unless the card specifies otherwise. Events take effect in the order played.

- **Harmful events** can be Calmed (ignored for one player) or negated (canceled for all).
- **Helpful events** can only be negated, not Calmed.
- Events go to the **Void** after being played.
- Events discarded from hand (before being played) go to the **Abyss**.

### Event Pause

When an event is played, other players have a chance to respond with one of:

- **Negate** the event (Limited Wish, Helm, Intercession)
- **Duplicate** the event (Bell of Might)
- **Calm** the event (Calm, Delsenora)
- **Deflect** the event (Deflection, Hijacking)

The decision to Calm or negate goes clockwise from the event player. Once passed, the opportunity is lost.

A player may attempt multiple negation methods if earlier attempts fail, but all actions must apply to the event — only one action per player applies.

### Caravan and Ancient Kalidnay

Neither may be used until after the last player in the first turn cycle has started their first turn.

---

## Order of Play

When a card is put into play, all players have a chance to respond before play continues. Playing quickly does not prevent responses.

A player activating a card already in play gets priority. If the order of play is unclear, the active player resolves their card first.

---

## Borrowing Cards

Borrowed cards are always returned to their original owner **at the end of the round of combat** they were borrowed, unless the card specifies otherwise or the borrowed card is discarded during combat.

---

## Copying Powers

When copying another card's special power:

- The exact text is copied.
- Avatar powers cannot be copied.
- Blueline rules and racial characteristics are also copied if in effect.
- The owner chooses a new card to copy each turn during Phase 3, unless stated otherwise.
- The copied power lasts for the card's stated duration, or until Phase 3 of the owning player's next turn, whichever is shorter.

---

## "All" or "Any" Cards

A champion with the ability to use cards of a particular type may use **all** cards of that type, including those with additional restrictions (e.g., "only usable by heroes"). The broader permission overrides narrower restrictions.

---

## Using Cards Already in Play

Any champion, magical item, or artifact that is voluntarily removed from play and brought back in the **same turn** loses its "voluntary" special power until end of turn. Immunities, race classifications, and other abilities remain.

---

## Counter-Effect Cards

Cards that counter or negate another card must be played **immediately** — as the next card played by the affected player. If any other card is played first, the window to counter is lost.

---

## Once-Per-Turn Items

"Once per turn" normally means once during Phase 3 of the owning player's turn. For items that respond to other actions (like the Bell of Might), the window is once between Phase 3 of the current turn and Phase 3 of the next turn.

Items that duplicate powers must select which power to copy during Phase 3 or immediately when played; the decision cannot be deferred.

---

## Special Cases and Clarifications

**Cards no longer in play** — once removed from the "in play" area, a card has no further effect on the game (long or short term).

**Flyers** — can attack any realm regardless of position. Allies must also be flyers (or swimmers/earthwalkers) to reach a protected realm with a flying attacker.

**Swimmers** — can attack any realm showing a coastline, regardless of position.

**Earthwalkers** — can attack any realm that doesn't have a specific movement restriction or champion type restriction, regardless of position.

> A flyer cannot choose not to fly. A swimmer cannot choose not to swim. An earthwalker cannot choose not to earthwalk.

**Avatars** — may only be brought into play during Phase 3. All conditions on the card must be met each time the avatar is played.

**Duplicated events and spells** — treated as normal separate cards. Can be dispelled, Calmed, or negated independently.

---

## Glossary

**Abyss** — A zone where certain spells and discarded (unplayed) events are sent. Cards can be recovered from the Abyss by specific cards.

**Adjusted Level** — The current level of a champion: base level plus all bonuses from attached cards. Unless specified otherwise, all references to "level" mean adjusted level.

**Allies** — Cards added to a champion during combat to increase adjusted level. Not considered champion types (hero, wizard, etc.) even if they look like one. Some allies are undead, dragons, flyers, earthwalkers, etc.

**Artifacts** — Attached only to a champion from the same world. One artifact per champion. Some can also be attached to realms of the same world. Not magical items.

**Attached Cards** — Magical items and artifacts attached to champions (or rarely realms) during Phase 3 or 4. Other cards (spells, blood abilities, thief abilities, etc.) can also be attached during combat. Magical items and artifacts return from combat with the victor; other attached cards are discarded at round end.

**Avatars** — God cards. Each requires specific cards to be sacrificed to bring them into play.

**Base Level** — The level printed on the card. Can be permanently altered by other cards. If another card adjusts the base level, the adjusted value becomes the new permanent base.

**Battle** — An attack upon a single realm during Phase 4. May last several rounds. Ends when the realm is razed/discarded, a defender discards an attacker, or the attacker declines to continue.

**Blood Abilities** — Used by regent champions. Either offensive or defensive, like spells.

**Calm** — Ignoring the effects of a harmful event for a single player. The event still affects other players.

**Champion** — A card with a wizard, hero, cleric, psionicist, regent, thief, or monster icon.

**Clerics** — Can cast cleric spells. Immune to Fear of Undead.

**Coast** — A designation for realms showing an ocean or sea. Coastal realms can be attacked by swimmers regardless of formation position.

**Combat Round** — One round of a battle: one attacker versus one defender.

**Defeated** — A champion is defeated when forced to discard from battle via cardplay, or when their adjusted level is lower and the owner can't or won't play more cards. Defeated champions normally go to the discard pile unless stated otherwise.

**Defensive** — A card power that affects only the card's own side, not the opposing card.

**Discarded** — Sent to the owner's discard pile. Synonyms in card text: killed, swallowed, devoured, slain. Being discarded causes defeat; being defeated doesn't always cause a discard.

**Dragons** — Champions and allies designated as dragons. Many are monsters, but can be any type.

**Draw Pile** — The face-down deck. When depleted, the discard pile is reshuffled into a new draw pile at end of turn.

**Dungeon Cards** — Free extra card (not counted in deck size). Placed into play at game start.

**Earthwalking** — Ability to attack any realm not restricted by special powers or specific movement types.

**Events** — Can be played at any time unless stated otherwise. After use, sent to the Void. If discarded unplayed, sent to the Abyss.

**Exposed Realm** — A realm that can be attacked — in the frontmost position, or behind only razed realms.

**Flyers** — Can attack any realm regardless of position. Flying is not automatically conferred to allies.

**Formation** — The pyramid of realm cards. Standard shape: 6 realms in a triangle (A at front, B-C in middle, D-E-F at back).

**Hand** — Cards held but not in play.

**Holdings** — Attached to realms during Phase 2. Must be from the same world as the realm. Cannot be rearranged once placed.

**Immunity** — Cannot be affected by a specific card type or effect. Immunities are always active. Unless noted, immunity applies only to offensive powers.

**In Play** — Cards in the pool, formation, involved in battle, or rule/event cards with stated duration. Razed realms are in play. Cards in Limbo, Abyss, or hand are not in play.

**Limbo** — Temporary removal zone. Champions normally return at end of their owner's next turn. Cards in Limbo are not in play. A champion sent to Limbo during combat loses all attachments.

**Magical Items** — Attached to any champion (multiple allowed). Each listed as defensive (affects only wielder's side) or offensive (affects opposing side). All magical items can be used when attacking or defending unless stated otherwise.

**Monsters** — Can use magical items and artifacts normally. No icon power.

**Offensive** — A card power that directly affects another card or the opposing player.

**Pool** — The in-play area where champions, artifacts, and magical items reside when not in battle. Right of the formation.

**Psionic Power Cards** — Used by psionicist champions. Either offensive or defensive. Can be canceled by other cards.

**Psionicists** — Can use psionic power cards as icon ability.

**Rarity** — Common, Uncommon, Rare, Chase (rarest). Realm-type cards are considered Common rarity.

**Razed Realm** — A realm flipped face-down. Remains in its formation position. Powers are inactive. Holdings attached to it are discarded. Still "in play" for Rule of the Cosmos.

**Realms** — Placed in the player's formation. Powers active only when the realm is being attacked, unless stated otherwise.

**Regents** — Can use blood ability cards.

**Removed from Game** — See Void.

**Round** — A single combat of attacker vs. defender.

**Rule Cards** — Played at Phase 0. Change rules for all players while in play. Playing a new rule card discards the current one.

**Spells** — Wizards cast wizard spells; clerics cast cleric spells. Each is offensive or defensive. Offensive/defensive is about effect direction, not who casts it — both types can be used when attacking or defending.

**Swimmers** — Can attack any coastal realm regardless of formation position.

**Thieves** — Can use thief skill cards.

**Thief Skills** — Either offensive or defensive. Used by thieves as icon ability.

**Undead** — Designated in the card's text. A card without the word "undead" is not undead regardless of appearance.

**Unarmed Combat Cards** — Generally usable by heroes, dragons, or undead. Either offensive or defensive.

**Void** — The removed-from-game area. Events go here after being played. Cards in the Void can never return.

**Weapon** — A designation for certain magical items. Gains additional bonuses from specific cards.

**Wizards** — Can cast wizard spells.

**World** — The origin of a realm, holding, champion, or artifact, noted by the logo near the card name. AD&D is its own world — not a generic world.
